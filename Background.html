Background and Significance of the Study

These days, the pandemic (COVID 19) which is now an endemic had affected multiple institutions. One of these being SMAC. This corporation aims to help children in need by giving them shelter, food, and proper education. Currently they are having trouble with educational funds; which is why we want to help them by executing this project. We plan to help them by creating infographics, posters, and promotional videos. So that we could help them gain sponsorships and donations.

Research methodology and description

We gathered crucial information/data, such as the age ranges of the individuals in the SMAC as well as their financial problems and current needs. Specifically, in the institution there are currently 2, 1-5 year olds, 18, 6-12 year olds, 6, 13-19 year olds, and 2, 20-50 year olds. From the interview with them, we also found out about their current problem with educational funds or just their funds in general which was because of the pandemic and not enough sponsors. They say that they plan to use the educational fund (if given) to create classrooms inside the institution with books, papers, pencils, and other materials. They say that if given the proper funding, it would greatly help the children to succeed in the future. In short, the data gathered via our efforts and the institutions, greatly sheds more light on their current situation and how we could help them as members of society and as ateneans.

We gathered this data by conducting an interview Aug 23, 2022 and data research from July 27, 2022 to October 25, 2022 to accurately get the latest and needed information. We contacted SMAC Sisters head Sister Luzviminda C. Pepito to gather the most recent information and needs as of the moment such as long-term issues the institution is dealing with, currently. We have talked through phone calls, emails, and text to ask questions for the interview and data gathering. We have also done our research throughout the internet, looking for articles and videos in which the SMAC Sisters are included.

We consider this data important in order to continue our research as it helps us in order to figure out the amount of funds and volunteers we may need in order to achieve these goals as well as be able to keep track of the items and resources we may need for the children such as medication, school supplies, clothes, daily necessities such as soap and food, and other necessities the children may need. This data can also help with letting us know how to ration items in case of an emergency, tell us how much resources we may need in order to apply certain projects into the institution and as well as let us be able to find the needed sponsorships we may need in order to do certain projects and certain tasks.
